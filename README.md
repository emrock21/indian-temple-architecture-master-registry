# Indian Temple Architecture Master Registry

This repository preserves, in an on-chain and censorship-resistant format, the architectural knowledge systems that shaped India’s temple-building traditions for more than 2,000 years. The registry documents the major schools of Indian temple architecture — Nagara, Dravida, Vesara, Kalinga, Hoysala, Kerala, Maru-Gurjara, and others — each with its own geometry, materials, sculptural programs, and cosmological symbolism.

## Why Temple Architecture Matters

Indian temples are not merely buildings; they are cosmological diagrams rendered in stone. Their forms encode:

- metaphysical concepts (axis mundi, Mount Meru, cosmic order)  
- dynastic aesthetics (Chola, Chandela, Hoysala, Solanki, Pandya)  
- regional materials (granite, sandstone, laterite, soapstone)  
- engineering innovations (star-shaped plans, monolithic vimanas, dry masonry)  
- sculptural languages (deities, guardians, narrative friezes, erotic symbolism)  

Each school represents a distinct worldview expressed through geometry, proportion, and iconography.

## Purpose of This Contract

The goal of this registry is to:

1. **Create a permanent, decentralized record** of India’s architectural lineages.  
2. **Standardize documentation** of temple styles using structured fields.  
3. **Preserve regional knowledge** that is often fragmented across archaeology, art history, and oral tradition.  
4. **Enable public contribution** of verified architectural data without centralized control.  
5. **Provide a transparent, immutable reference** for researchers, historians, architects, and cultural institutions.

This contract is intentionally:

- **ownerless** — no authority can alter or censor the registry  
- **payment-free** — no fees, no monetization, no barriers  
- **immutable** — once recorded, entries become part of the cultural ledger  
- **minimalistic and secure** — no external calls, no complexity, no attack surface  

## What Each Entry Records

Every architectural style is documented through:

- **region** — geographic and cultural zone  
- **school or dynasty** — Chola, Hoysala, Chandela, Solanki, etc.  
- **style name** — Dravida Vimana, Rekha-Deul, Star-Plan, etc.  
- **materials** — granite, sandstone, soapstone, laterite  
- **plan and elevation** — sanctum geometry, tower type, mandapa layout  
- **iconography** — deities, sculptural programs, narrative cycles  
- **uniqueness** — what makes the style distinct  

This structure allows the registry to capture both technical and symbolic dimensions of temple architecture.
